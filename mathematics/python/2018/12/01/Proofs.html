Overview

We must be able to speak and write about mathematics in order to do mathematics. That seem sort of circular doesn't it. What I mean is that formal mathematical reasoning, which includes mathematical definitions, statements, and proofs are the language in which mathemtics is written.

Mathematics entails more than just seeking solutions to problems. Writing will become more important as we progress to more advanced and abstract mathematics.

Proof

A proof is a method for ascertaning the truth.

How is truth established:

  • Experiment and observation
  • Sampling and counter examples
  • Judge, jury
  • Word of God

A mathematical proof is a verication of a proposition by a chain of logical deductions from a set of of axioms.

Proposions

A proposition is a statement that is either true or false

Examples of simple propositions

2 + 2 = 4

$\forall n\ \in \mathbb{N}, n^2+n=41$ is a prime number

The first example, $2 + 2 = 4$, . Reading the second example in English:For every nonnegative integer, $n$, the value of $n^2+n=41$ is prime. Lets break down the sentence structure and what these symbols mean before we continue.

  • $\forall$: for all, or 'for every possible choice of $n$

  • $\in$: is a member of; belongs to; is in.

  • $\mathbb{N}$: The set of Natural numbers, {0, 1, 2, 3, ...}

  • $n^2+n=41$ is a prime number: this part is called the Predicate, a proposition whose truth depends on the value of the variable. 1
